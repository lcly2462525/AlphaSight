# NVDA: AI Hype Is Peaking, But Inventory Risk and Diversification Pressures Signal a Near-Term Top

**Stance: NVDA is priced for perfection and likely to underperform over the next 6–12 months due to rising inventory provisions, signs of client diversification away from GPU dependency, and a price run-up that has outpaced even its strong earnings momentum.**

NVIDIA’s financial performance in fiscal 2026 remains exceptional, with FY2026Q3 revenue reaching $147.8 billion and net income hitting $77.1 billion, up sharply from $90.8 billion in revenue and $45.2 billion in net income in Q2 [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]. Earnings per share have consistently beat expectations: $1.30 actual vs. $1.2746 estimate in Q3 (1.99% surprise), following a $1.05 actual vs. $1.0281 estimate in Q2 (2.13% surprise) [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"]. These results confirm that demand for AI accelerators remains robust and that NVDA continues to dominate the high-end GPU market.

However, the financials also reveal a growing risk: inventory provisions. In the first nine months of FY2026, NVDA recorded $3.6 billion in inventory provisions, a 311% increase from $876 million in the same period of FY2025 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively, and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. This surge suggests either overproduction, product transitions, or weakening demand visibility—particularly concerning given that inventory write-downs directly reduce gross margin and signal potential supply-demand imbalances.

Simultaneously, evidence of strategic pushback from major cloud clients is mounting. Meta Platforms is now developing its own AI chip, reducing long-term reliance on NVDA [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "$META is joining $GOOG and $AMZN as it starts using its own AI chip. ... They wouldn’t stay dependent on $NVDA forever."]. Alphabet and Amazon have already begun similar in-house efforts, indicating a structural shift: hyperscalers are moving to control their AI stack, including silicon. This trend threatens NVDA’s recurring revenue model, which depends on continuous GPU refresh cycles and pricing power.

Market sentiment, meanwhile, appears euphoric. The launch of the Tuttle Capital Magnificent 7 Income Blast ETF (MAGO), which includes NVDA as a core holding, reflects institutional demand for yield-generating exposure to the "Magnificent 7" [SOURCE: news/NVDA/137940083.json | "MAGO is an actively managed exchange-traded fund that seeks to provide current income and exposure to the equity performance of the 'Magnificent 7'"]. Additionally, NVDA’s stock price rose 34.8% from $138.31 on January 2, 2025, to $186.50 by December 31, 2025 [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]. This run-up occurred alongside a broader thematic basket—the “OpenAI complex”—that gained ~90–100% by early October 2025 [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-25.json | "ramping to roughly +40‑50% by July and to about +90‑100% by early October."].

Yet, cracks are emerging. By late November 2025, NVDA was “getting punished” even as peers like Broadcom hit new all-time highs [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "$NVDA getting punished. ... Meanwhile… $AVGO smashed a new ATH before earnings"]. This divergence suggests investors are rotating from pure-play AI enablers to companies with more diversified exposure or better near-term visibility.

Furthermore, NVDA’s capital return program, while substantial, has not offset valuation concerns. The company repurchased $9.7 billion of stock in Q2 FY2026, up from $7.0 billion in the same quarter the prior year [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "We repurchased $ 9.7 billion and $ 7.0 billion during the second quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. It also paid $244 million in dividends in Q2, consistent with prior periods [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "We paid cash dividends to our shareholders of $ 244 million and $ 246 million during the second quarter..."]. But aggressive buybacks cannot indefinitely support the stock if fundamentals decelerate.

**Cross-source chain**: Rising inventory provisions (filing) → signal potential demand softness → coincide with client in-sourcing trends (social) → contribute to price underperformance relative to peers (price data) → despite strong EPS beats (earnings). This sequence shows that even strong earnings are no longer sufficient to drive new highs.

**Falsification test**: This thesis would be invalidated if NVDA’s inventory provisions decline meaningfully in FY2026Q4 while revenue growth accelerates above 50% QoQ and at least two new hyperscalers announce major GPU procurement deals—evidence that demand is re-accelerating and inventory was merely precautionary. Absent such developments, the confluence of valuation, inventory risk, and client diversification points to a near-term peak.

**Conclusion**: NVDA’s dominance in AI computing is undeniable, but the stock is now priced for flawless execution. With inventory write-downs surging, key clients building competitive chips, and the share price already reflecting extreme optimism, the risk-reward is skewed to the downside. We expect NVDA to underperform the broader Magnificent 7 cohort over the next six months.
