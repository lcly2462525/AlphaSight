# H20 Inventory Charge and China Revenue Headwinds Are Priced-In; Neutral 20-Day Return Outlook

NVIDIA’s $4.5 billion H20 inventory charge in FY26 Q1 and minimal China data center revenue recovery are fully reflected in current valuation, and we expect NVDA to deliver flat to slightly negative relative performance versus the SOX over the next 20 trading days following the earnings release window (April–May 2025). The market has already priced in both the near-term structural exclusion from China’s data center market and the financial impact of the inventory write-down, as evidenced by prior price action and forward guidance embedded in filings.

The $4.5 billion charge associated with H20 excess inventory and purchase obligations was explicitly recognized in the first quarter of fiscal 2026:  
`[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "As a result of these new requirements, we incurred a $4.5 billion charge in the first quarter of fiscal year 2026 associated with H20 for excess inventory and purchase obligations"]`.  
This non-recurring charge materially impacted FY26 Q1 net income of $18.775 billion on revenue of $44.062 billion:  
`[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 revenue=44062000000.0 net_income=18775000000.0"]`.  
Absent the charge, underlying profitability would have been approximately $23.3 billion, implying a one-time reduction of ~19% in quarterly net income. However, the charge was announced contemporaneously with the April 2025 U.S. export license requirement, allowing investors time to anticipate the hit.

Despite limited resumption of H20 shipments under U.S. licenses granted in August 2025, actual revenue recognized remains negligible. As of the November 2025 10-Q, NVDA had generated only “approximately $50 million in H20 revenue under those licenses”:  
`[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "to date, we have generated approximately $50 million in H20 revenue under those licenses"]`.  
This represents just 0.11% of Q1 revenue and confirms that China’s contribution to data center sales remains structurally impaired. Moreover, NVDA acknowledges it is “unable to create and deliver a competitive product for China’s data center market” under current rules:  
`[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Under the current rules and geopolitical landscape, we are unable to create and deliver a competitive product for China’s data center computing/compute market"]`.  
The company further states it has been “effectively foreclosed from competing in China's data center computing/compute market”:  
`[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We have effectively been foreclosed from competing in China's data center computing/compute market"]`,  
validating long-term revenue attrition risk.

Market sentiment, as reflected in social media commentary, initially reacted positively to rumors of resumed China sales. On December 8, 2025, tweets noted “$NVDA +2.5% in post-market” amid reports that “China can receive H200 products for approved customers”:  
`[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-08.json | "$NVDA +2.5% in post-market https://t.co/c95y1PuTdg"]`.  
However, these gains were speculative and disconnected from actual revenue realization. By contrast, more informed commentary on August 10, 2025, highlighted margin pressure from the reported 15% U.S. government revenue share requirement:  
`[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-08-10.json | "giving up 15% of China chip sales hits margins immediately out the freaking gate"]`.  
This aligns with NVDA’s own disclosure that “USG officials have expressed an expectation that the USG will receive 15% or more of the revenue generated from licensed sales”:  
`[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "USG officials have expressed an expectation that the USG will receive 15% or more of the revenue generated from licensed sales"]`,  
further constraining profitability even on minimal shipments.

Price action over 2025 supports the conclusion that downside risks are priced-in. NVDA rose from $138.31 on January 2, 2025, to $186.50 by December 31, 2025, a 34.8% increase:  
`[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]`.  
This advance occurred despite the known H20 export restrictions announced in April and the $4.5 billion charge, indicating investors factored in China-related headwinds while focusing on strength in global AI demand and cloud partnerships.

We therefore assert a **falsifiable stance**: NVDA’s stock will underperform the SOX by ≤1% over the 20 trading days following FY26 Q1 earnings (April–May 2025), because the H20 charge and China revenue weakness are already priced-in. A move of >2% outperformance versus SOX would falsify this thesis, implying renewed optimism about China revenue recovery not supported by current filing disclosures.

Conclusion: The H20 inventory charge and China revenue volatility are **priced-in**. No material re-rating catalyst exists in the near term. Maintain neutral relative stance.
