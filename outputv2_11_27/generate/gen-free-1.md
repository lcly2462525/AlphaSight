# NVDA: Growth Still Intact, But Margin Pressures Signal Caution Ahead

**Stance: Neutral (Overvalued near-term, but structural growth intact)**

NVIDIA (NVDA) continues to deliver exceptional top-line and earnings growth in fiscal 2026, validating its central role in the AI infrastructure buildout. However, rising inventory provisions and early signs of competitive pressure from hyperscalers developing in-house AI chips suggest that near-term margin expansion may be capped. While the company’s financial performance remains strong, recent price action and thematic ETF flows appear to have pulled forward substantial optimism, leaving limited room for multiple expansion. We maintain a neutral stance, arguing that NVDA is fairly valued at current levels, with upside contingent on margin stabilization and sustained demand outpacing supply chain investments.

Revenue growth remains explosive, with NVDA reporting $147.8 billion in revenue for FY2026Q3, up from $90.8 billion in Q2 and $44.1 billion in Q1 [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]. Net income has scaled proportionally, reaching $77.1 billion in Q3, up from $45.2 billion in Q2 and $18.8 billion in Q1, reflecting strong operating leverage [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]. EPS has consistently beat estimates: $1.30 actual vs. $1.2746 expected in Q3 (surprise: +1.99%) [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"], following beats of +2.13% in Q2 and +0.83% in Q1 [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q2 EPS actual=1.05 est=1.0281 surprise=0.0219 (2.1301%)"]. This trajectory confirms robust execution and sustained demand for Hopper and Blackwell architecture GPUs.

Yet, cost structure trends raise caution. The company recorded $378 million in inventory provisions in Q3 of FY2026, up from $322 million in the same quarter last year [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. More significantly, cumulative provisions for the first nine months of FY2026 reached $3.6 billion, a 311% increase from $876 million in the prior-year period [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. This sharp rise suggests either supply chain overhang, potential obsolescence risk, or strategic stockpiling ahead of next-gen transitions — all of which pressure gross margins and signal operational complexity ahead.

Concurrently, competitive dynamics are shifting. Hyperscalers are reducing reliance on NVDA: “$META is joining $GOOG and $AMZN as it starts using its own AI chip. ... They wouldn’t stay dependent on $NVDA forever” [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "$META is joining $GOOG and $AMZN as it starts using its own AI chip. ... They wouldn’t stay dependent on $NVDA forever."]. While NVDA remains the dominant enabler of training workloads, the long-term risk of customer-owned silicon capturing inference or specialized workloads is materializing. This is corroborated by the divergence in thematic baskets: while the “OpenAI complex” (including NVDA) rose ~90–100% by early October 2025, recent rotation favors hardware suppliers like Broadcom ($AVGO), which “smashed a new ATH before earnings” [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "Meanwhile… $AVGO smashed a new ATH before earnings"], suggesting investors are pricing in broader AI infrastructure beneficiaries beyond just NVDA.

Price performance reflects this bifurcation. NVDA shares rose from $138.31 on January 2, 2025, to $186.50 by December 31, a 34.8% return [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]. This outperformance occurred amid strong earnings and thematic ETF creation, such as the Tuttle Capital Magnificent 7 Income Blast ETF (MAGO), which includes NVDA as a core holding [SOURCE: news/NVDA/137940083.json | "MAGO is an actively managed exchange-traded fund... exposure to the equity performance of the 'Magnificent 7' - Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta Platforms, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Tesla"]. However, the surge appears increasingly detached from incremental fundamentals: the stock’s P/E on forward FY2026 EPS is not available in the provided corpus, but the magnitude of price appreciation relative to earnings surprise (max +2.13%) suggests multiple expansion has been the primary driver.

**Falsification test**: Our neutral stance would be invalidated if NVDA’s inventory provisions decline meaningfully in FY2026Q4 while revenue growth remains above 30% QoQ and hyperscalers reaffirm major new GPU commitments. Conversely, if provisions exceed $400 million in Q4 or a major cloud provider announces a significant shift to in-house inference chips, the stock would warrant a downgrade to *underweight*.
