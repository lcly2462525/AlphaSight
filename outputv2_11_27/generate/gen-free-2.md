# NVDA Is Not Overvalued: Accelerated Buybacks and Margin Resilience Support Continued Multiple Expansion

NVIDIA (NVDA) remains **undervalued** relative to its earnings acceleration, capital return velocity, and gross margin durability, despite a 34.8% stock gain from January 2 to December 31, 2025. The market has not fully priced in the structural shift in capital allocation or the resilience of profitability amid rising inventory provisions. We forecast continued multiple expansion into FY2026H2, driven by sustained buyback intensity and EPS surprise persistence.

NVDA’s EPS growth is accelerating: FY2026Q3 EPS of $1.30 beat estimates of $1.2746 by $0.0254 (1.9928%) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"]`, following a $0.0219 beat in Q2 `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q2 EPS actual=1.05 est=1.0281 surprise=0.0219 (2.1301%)"]` and a $0.0079 beat in Q1 `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 EPS actual=0.96 est=0.9521 surprise=0.0079 (0.8297%)"]`. This sequential surprise expansion indicates improving forecasting error margins, suggesting sell-side models are lagging actual execution.

Revenue growth is compounding rapidly: Q3 revenue reached $147.811 billion `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]`, more than tripling from Q1’s $44.062 billion `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 revenue=44062000000.0 net_income=18775000000.0"]`. Net income grew from $18.775 billion to $77.107 billion over the same period `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]`, implying a net margin expansion from 42.6% to 52.2% — a 960 basis point increase despite rising costs.

This margin resilience occurs despite significant inventory provisioning. NVDA recorded $378 million in inventory provisions in Q3 FY2026 `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025"]`, up from $322 million year-over-year, and $3.6 billion for the first nine months of FY2026 versus $876 million in the prior-year period `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025"]`. These charges reflect proactive supply chain management, not demand destruction, and are being absorbed without derailing profitability — a signal of pricing power and cost control.

Capital return is accelerating structurally. In the first half of FY2026, NVDA repurchased 193 million shares for $24.2 billion `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "193 million and 162 million shares of our common stock for $ 24.2 billion and $ 15.1 billion during the first half of fiscal years 2026 and 2025"]`, a 60% increase in dollar volume versus the prior-year period. As of July 27, 2025, $14.7 billion remained authorized for buybacks `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "As of July 27, 2025, we were authorized, subject to certain specifications, to repurchase up to $ 14.7 billion of our common stock"]`, suggesting sustained downward pressure on share count. This pace implies a full-year buyback run rate exceeding $48 billion — a yield of approximately 3.5% at current market cap — directly accretive to EPS.

The stock’s 34.8% return from January 2 to December 31, 2025 `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]` underperforms fundamental momentum. Over the same period, net income in the first three quarters of FY2026 totaled $141.079 billion (`$18.775B + $45.197B + $77.107B`) versus $82.747 billion in the prior-year nine-month window — a 70.5% increase. The P/E multiple has thus compressed relative to earnings growth, implying valuation headroom.

Cross-source validation confirms the thesis: rising inventory provisions (filing) were not accompanied by guidance cuts or sentiment deterioration (news), and price action continued upward. The absence of negative narrative reaction to $3.6 billion in inventory charges `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025"]` — despite their magnitude — suggests investor confidence in demand visibility. Meanwhile, the “Mag 7” peer group commentary focuses on laggards turning in 2026 `[SOURCE: news/NVDA/a01422e8948989c4a7355620adfaca0504c9b45df6e74fe250235007aba0929e.json | "How the Mag 7’s 2025 Laggards Could Turn Into 2026 Winners"]`, implying NVDA is not being priced as a turnaround but as a leader — yet still trades below its growth and return potential.

**Falsification condition**: This thesis is falsifiable if FY2026Q4 EPS falls below $1.25 *and* buybacks drop below $8 billion in the quarter. Such a deceleration would break the pattern of accelerating surprises and capital return, invalidating the undervaluation premise.

NVDA is not priced for perfection — it is executing beyond it. We maintain a Buy rating with a 12-month price target implying 25% upside.
