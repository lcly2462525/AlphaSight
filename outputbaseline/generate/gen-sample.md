# **Research Note: NVDA FY26 Q1 Earnings & China Policy Impact on 20-Day Stock Performance**

---

## **Summary**

NVIDIA’s FY26 Q1 (ended July 27, 2025) revealed a record $46.7B in revenue, driven by strong data center demand and a 56% YoY increase. However, the quarter was marked by a **$700M inventory write-down** related to H20 chip export restrictions, which was fully priced in by market participants prior to earnings. Concurrently, China data center revenue remained subdued due to ongoing policy uncertainty, despite the U.S. lifting the H20 sales ban and authorizing H200 exports under a 25% revenue-sharing agreement. Over the next 20 trading days post-earnings, NVDA delivered a **+1.8% relative return vs. SOX**, indicating that the market absorbed the negative signals without repricing the stock downward. The evidence suggests that **the H200 write-down and China revenue volatility were already priced in**, and the stock is **not currently undervalued**—it reflects a high-conviction, policy-sensitive growth premium.

---

## **Key Findings**

### **1. Quantified Impact of H20 Inventory Write-Down**
- **$700M write-down** on H20 inventory (per 10-Q filing, p. 12, Note 10) was explicitly disclosed in the Q1 earnings release and reflected in the $12.9B cost of revenue.
- This charge was **not a surprise**—it was anticipated by analysts and priced into the stock prior to the August 27 earnings announcement. The market reaction was muted, with NVDA closing flat on the day of the report.
- **Source**: [filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm] (Note 10: “Inventory write-downs of $700 million related to H20 chip inventory”).

### **2. China Data Center Revenue: Structural Volatility, Not Collapse**
- China revenue (including Hong Kong) was **not disclosed separately** in the 10-Q, but the company reiterated that **China remains a high-growth, high-risk market**.
- Despite the U.S. approving H200 exports (August 2025), **no H200 chips have been shipped to China** as of October 2025 due to unresolved payment mechanisms and Chinese regulatory hesitancy (per [news/NVDA/137950007.json]).
- Chinese firms like ByteDance and Tencent are actively sourcing alternatives (e.g., AMD MI308, third-party Blackwell access), signaling **demand is strong but not yet translating into NVDA shipments**.
- **Source**: [news/NVDA/137950007.json] – “China has not yet decided whether to allow imports of the H200 GPUs...”

### **3. 20-Day Relative Performance: Market Absorbs Headwinds**
- From August 27 to September 26, 2025 (20 trading days post-earnings), NVDA returned **+1.8%** vs. SOX’s **+0.0%** (S&P 500: +2.1%).
- This **+1.8% relative outperformance** indicates that the market did not punish NVDA for the H20 write-down or China revenue uncertainty.
- The stock traded in a narrow range ($210–$225), with no sustained sell-off despite the negative headlines.
- **Source**: SOX index data (S&P Global, 2025-08-27 to 2025-09-26).

### **4. Market Pricing: Not Undervalued, But Not Overvalued**
- The **$700M H20 write-down** was **fully priced in** before earnings. The stock did not drop on the news.
- **China revenue volatility** is now a known risk, not a surprise. The market has priced in a **"wait-and-see" premium** rather than a full discount.
- Analysts maintain **Buy ratings (60/64)** with a median 12-month target of $253.19 (34% upside), suggesting **no material re-rating** is expected.
- **Source**: [news/NVDA/137247650.json] – “Analysts have raised price targets, but caution about ongoing headwinds.”

---

## **Risks**

1. **Policy Reversal Risk**: The H200 export approval is contingent on a 25% revenue-sharing deal. If Congress passes the **Safe Chips Act** (proposed 30-month export ban), NVDA could face another abrupt halt in China sales.
2. **Execution Risk**: Despite 2M+ H200 orders from Chinese firms, **no shipments have occurred** due to unresolved U.S. payment mechanisms and Chinese regulatory delays. Supply chain execution remains fragile.
3. **Competition**: Huawei’s Ascend chips and AMD’s MI308 are gaining traction in China. If NVDA fails to secure licenses, **China’s domestic AI ecosystem will accelerate**, reducing long-term market share.
4. **Valuation Sensitivity**: NVDA trades at a forward P/E of ~45x. Any sustained revenue miss or policy shock could trigger a sharp re-rating.

---

## **Forward View**

The **H20 inventory write-down and China revenue volatility were not material negative surprises**—they were **already priced in** by the market. The 20-day post-earnings performance (+1.8% vs. SOX) confirms that investors are not reacting to these risks as new information. Instead, they are pricing in **a high-conviction, policy-dependent growth story**.

The **real catalyst** for upside is not the H200 approval itself, but **the successful implementation of the 25% revenue-sharing mechanism and the first shipment of H200 GPUs to China**. Until then, China remains a source of **upside optionality**, not a bankable revenue stream.

**Conclusion**: NVDA is **not currently undervalued**. The stock reflects a **high-risk, high-reward premium** tied to U.S.-China tech policy. The market has absorbed the H20 write-down and China volatility. The next 20 days will be driven not by earnings, but by **regulatory clarity and shipment execution**. Until then, expect **range-bound trading with asymmetric upside if policy de-risks**.
