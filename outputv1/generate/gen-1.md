# NVDA’s Outperformance in FY26 Q1–Q3 Was Driven by Blackwell Demand Surge, Not H20 China Exit

NVDA’s stock outperformed the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) by +34.8% from Jan 2, 2025, to Dec 31, 2025, despite a $4.5 billion H20 inventory charge in FY26 Q1 and zero China revenue in Q2 and Q3 [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv][SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. The true driver was not the absence of China revenue, but the **massive, quantifiable surge in Blackwell demand**, which more than offset the H20-related headwinds. The thesis is falsifiable: if Blackwell revenue growth had been below 15% sequentially in Q2 and Q3, or if H20-related charges exceeded $6 billion cumulatively, the outperformance would be unexplained by fundamentals.

The $4.5 billion H20 charge in FY26 Q1 was a one-time, non-cash write-down tied to excess inventory and purchase obligations after U.S. export controls restricted H20 sales to China-based customers [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm][SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. This charge was fully recognized in Q1, with no further material H20-related provisions in Q2 or Q3 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. Despite this, NVDA’s Data Center revenue grew 56% YoY in Q2 and 56% YoY in Q3, with compute revenue rising 27% sequentially in Q3 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm][SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. This growth was entirely attributable to Blackwell, which grew 17% sequentially in Q2 and became the leading architecture across all customer categories by Q3 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm][SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm].

The scale of Blackwell’s contribution is critical: in Q3, Data Center compute revenue reached $43.0 billion, up 27% from Q2’s $33.9 billion [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json][SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. This implies a Blackwell revenue increase of $9.1 billion in Q3 alone. In contrast, the H20-related revenue loss was $4.0 billion in Q2, with no sales in Q3 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. The net impact of H20 exit was a $4.0 billion revenue drag in Q2 and zero in Q3, while Blackwell added $9.1 billion in Q3. The **net revenue gain from architecture transition was $5.1 billion**, far exceeding the $4.5 billion charge.

Furthermore, Blackwell’s demand was not limited to cloud providers. Gaming revenue rose 30% YoY in Q3, driven by Blackwell adoption [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. Professional Visualization revenue grew 18% sequentially, fueled by Blackwell in notebooks for AI workflows [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. Automotive revenue rose 69% YoY, underpinned by self-driving platforms [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. These segments were unaffected by China export controls and contributed to the revenue surge.

The cross-source chain is clear:  
1. **Filing event**: U.S. export controls restricted H20 sales to China (April 2025) [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm].  
2. **Financial impact**: $4.5 billion H20 charge in Q1 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm].  
3. **Market reaction**: Stock rose 34.8% over the year despite the charge, with EPS surprises of 0.96% (Q1), 1.05% (Q2), and 1.30% (Q3) [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json].  
4. **Falsification test**: If Blackwell revenue growth had been below 15% in Q2 and Q3, or if H20 charges exceeded $6 billion, the stock’s outperformance would be unexplained by fundamentals and the thesis would be invalid.

In conclusion, NVDA’s outperformance was not due to the China exit being “priced in,” but because the **Blackwell demand surge ($9.1B Q3 revenue) dwarfed the H20 exit impact ($4.0B Q2 revenue loss)**. The $4.5 billion charge was a one-time accounting event, while Blackwell’s momentum was structural and accelerating. The stock’s performance reflects a market pricing in a fundamental shift to a higher-margin, higher-growth architecture—**not a reaction to a China revenue loss**.
