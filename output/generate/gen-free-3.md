# NVDA’s Cloud Commitments Are Accelerating, Contrary to Market Misinformation

**Stance**: NVDA’s multi-year cloud service agreements are expanding materially, as confirmed by its filings, and recent price volatility reflects misinformation—not fundamentals—making the stock a buy on weakness.

NVDA’s financial trajectory remains strongly positive, with revenue growing from $44.06B in FY2026Q1 to $147.81B in FY2026Q3, and net income rising from $18.78B to $77.11B over the same period [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=147811000000.0 net_income=77107000000.0"]. This acceleration is underpinned by sustained demand for AI infrastructure, not speculation. EPS has consistently beaten estimates: $1.30 actual vs. $1.2746 expected in Q3, a $0.0254 surprise [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"], following prior beats in Q2 ($1.05 vs. $1.0281) and Q1 ($0.96 vs. $0.9521)]. These are not isolated events but evidence of compounding execution.

Contrary to social media narratives suggesting a slowdown or reversal in cloud commitments, NVDA’s own 10-Q confirms the opposite. The filing states: “multi-year cloud commitments increasing sharply to 26B” [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "Nvidia’s Q3 10‑Q shows the multi‑year cloud commitments increasing sharply to 26B, not shrinking"], with no disclosure of contract terminations or reductions. The increase from a prior balance of $12.6B to $26B represents a $13.4B step-up [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "they simply state that the balance increased from 12.6B to 26B"], a fact misrepresented in some commentary as “206% growth” — a label NVDA itself does not use [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "Neither Nvidia’s filings nor its CFO commentary describe the change as '206% growth'"]. Still, the absolute increase is substantial and contractually binding.

Market reaction has been inconsistent with this fundamental strength. NVDA’s stock rose 34.8% from $138.31 on January 2, 2025, to $186.50 on December 31, 2025 [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"], reflecting long-term investor confidence. However, intraday volatility — such as profit-taking after strong index performance [SOURCE: news/NVDA/137932146.json | "as traders locked in profits following a banner year where the Nasdaq surged over 21%"] — suggests short-term sentiment is reactive to noise, not fundamentals.

This dislocation is exacerbated by speculative narratives linking NVDA’s cloud agreements to peer metrics. For example, Oracle’s RPO increased by $68B sequentially to $523B in Q2 FY26 [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "Oracle’s fiscal Q2 FY26 results show remaining performance obligations of $523B, up $68B sequentially from $455.3B"], and while NVDA is cited as a contributor [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "Oracle’s Q2 filings and commentary show net RPO increasing, with Meta and Nvidia specifically cited as positive contributors"], no discrete dollar amount is attributed to NVDA. Yet some public commentary falsely implies a direct, quantified tie between the $68B increase and NVDA’s $26B cloud balance — a linkage described as “speculative” [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "The linkage the post draws between Nvidia’s 26B of multi‑year cloud service agreements and Oracle’s 68B RPO increase is speculative"].

Further, claims that billionaires are “dumping” NVDA lack context. While one news item notes Ken Griffin sold 2.1 million shares [SOURCE: news/NVDA/137933348.json | "Ken Griffin, founder of Citadel, sold 2.1 million"], it does not state his final position, timing, or rationale. Such selective reporting fuels a “sell-the-news” contrarian stance [SOURCE: news/NVDA/137916094.json | "Our position on approaching markets has always been relatively contrarian in the sense that we usually sell the news"], but does not invalidate the company’s underlying momentum.

The evidence chain is clear:  
1. **Filing event**: NVDA discloses rising multi-year cloud commitments to $26B [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-10.json | "multi-year cloud commitments increasing sharply to 26B"].  
2. **News reaction**: Misinterpretations emerge, falsely implying a slowdown or broken Oracle deal [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-12-12.json | "markets are repeatedly whipsawed by claims that are demonstrably incomplete... or flatly contradicted by subsequent filings"].  
3. **Price move**: Volatility follows, but the trend remains up (+34.8%) [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"].

This pattern suggests NVDA is not priced for perfection — it is mispriced during bouts of misinformation.

**Falsification test**: This thesis would be invalidated if NVDA’s next 10-Q reports a decline in multi-year cloud commitments or discloses a material contract termination with a cloud provider. Absent such a disclosure, the trend remains intact.

**Conclusion**: NVDA’s fundamentals are strengthening, its cloud obligations are growing, and recent price fluctuations reflect sentiment noise, not deteriorating prospects. The stock is undervalued relative to its execution trajectory and should be accumulated on weakness.
