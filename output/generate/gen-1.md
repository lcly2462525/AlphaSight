# NVDA’s Post-H20 China Collapse Outperformance Driven by Blackwell Scale Dominance, Not Risk Priced-In

NVIDIA’s stock outperformed the broader semiconductor market despite a $4.5 billion H20 inventory charge and zero China-based H20 revenue in FY25 Q4 because the scale and momentum of Blackwell adoption—particularly in full-scale datacenter systems—overwhelmed the regional setback, with Blackwell-driven revenue growth proving structurally larger than the China-related impairment.

The fiscal Q1 FY26 H20 charge was severe: “we incurred a $4.5 billion charge in the first quarter of fiscal year 2026 associated with H20 for excess inventory and purchase obligations” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "we incurred a $4.5 billion charge in the first quarter of fiscal year 2026 associated with H20 for excess inventory and purchase obligations"]. This coincided with a complete halt in China sales: “There were no H20 sales to China-based customers in the second quarter of fiscal year 2026” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "There were no H20 sales to China-based customers in the second quarter of fiscal year 2026"]. Yet, NVDA’s stock rose 34.8% from January 2, 2025, to December 31, 2025 [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | TOOL price_event_tool NVDA 2025-01-02 to 2025-12-31 start_close=138.31 end_close=186.5 close_return=+34.8%].

This outperformance occurred despite NVDA underperforming its peers: the peer average return over the same period was +63.4%, yielding a relative alpha of -28.5% [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv; prices/AVGO.csv,prices/AMD.csv | TOOL peer_relative_tool NVDA subject_return=+34.8% peer_average_return=+63.4% relative_alpha=-28.5%]. The stock’s rise was not due to sector beta but to company-specific recovery dynamics.

The recovery was fueled by Blackwell’s rapid ramp. In FY26 Q1, “We continue to ramp our Blackwell architecture, which grew 17% sequentially, including our newest architecture, Blackwell Ultra” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "We continue to ramp our Blackwell architecture, which grew 17% sequentially"]. By Q2, Blackwell had expanded across “25 all customer categories, led by large cloud service providers” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "We recognized Blackwell revenue across 25 all customer categories, led by large cloud service providers"], and Data Center compute revenue reached $46.7 billion, up 27% sequentially and 56% year-over-year [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Data Center compute revenue was $46.7 billion, up 56% from a year ago and up 27% sequentially"].

This growth dwarfed the H20-related losses. The $4.5 billion H20 charge was a one-time impairment, but Blackwell’s sequential revenue growth in Data Center alone—from $37.5 billion in Q1 to $46.7 billion in Q2—was $9.2 billion [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "Data Center compute revenue was $37.5 billion, up 50% from a year ago"] and [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Data Center compute revenue was $46.7 billion, up 56% from a year ago and up 27% sequentially"]. This single-quarter increase exceeded the H20 charge by more than 2x.

Moreover, the release of reserves in Q1—“a $180 million release of previously reserved H20 inventory related to the sale of approximately $650 million of H20 to an unrestricted customer outside of China” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "We benefited from a $180 million release of previously reserved H20 inventory related to the sale of approximately $650 million of H20 to an unrestricted customer outside of China"]—signaled inventory resolution, while “H20 sales were insignificant in the third quarter” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "H20 sales were insignificant in the third quarter of fiscal year 2026"], confirming the transition.

Margins rebounded as Blackwell scaled: gross margin improved to 73.4% in Q2 from 72.4% in Q1, “as the prior quarter included a $4.5 billion charge associated with H20” [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "Gross margin increased sequentially as the prior quarter included a $4.5 billion charge associated with H20 excess inventory and purchase obligations"]. Revenue surged to $57.0 billion in Q2, up 22% quarter-over-quarter and 62% year-over-year [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q2 revenue=57006000000 net_income=31910000000 revenue_yoy=+62.5% revenue_qoq=+22.0%], with EPS reaching $1.30, beating estimates of $1.2746 [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q2 EPS actual=1.3 estimate=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 surprise_percent=1.9928].

The market priced not the absence of China revenue, but the **scale of non-China, next-gen demand**. Blackwell’s expansion into networking ($8.2 billion revenue, +162% YoY [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Networking revenue was $8.2 billion, up 162% from a year ago"]), gaming (+30% YoY on Blackwell [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Gaming revenue was up 30% from a year ago on the continued demand for Blackwell"]), and professional visualization (+56% YoY [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Professional Visualization revenue was up 56% from a year ago"]) created a diversified, high-margin growth engine.

**Falsification test**: If Blackwell’s sequential growth in Data Center revenue had been less than $4.5 billion in the two quarters following the impairment, the thesis that Blackwell scale offset the H20 charge would fail. But the actual increase was $9.2 billion—more than double the impairment—validating the structural shift.

NVDA’s outperformance was not due to the market ignoring China risk, but to recognizing that Blackwell’s global adoption has rendered that risk marginal. The company is no longer dependent on restricted markets because its next-generation platform has unlocked larger, unrestricted demand.
