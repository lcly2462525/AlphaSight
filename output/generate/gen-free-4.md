# NVDA’s Growth Premium Is No Longer Justified: Earnings Momentum Falters Amid Capital Discipline Concerns

**Stance: NVDA is overvalued relative to its near-term earnings trajectory and capital allocation efficiency, despite continued strong revenue growth. The stock’s 34.8% return in 2025 underperformed its peers and is decoupled from deteriorating margin trends and ballooning inventory provisions — a combination that falsifies the assumption of sustained high-quality earnings expansion.**

NVDA delivered FY2026Q3 revenue of $57.0 billion, a 62.5% year-over-year increase and 22.0% quarter-over-quarter acceleration, confirming continued demand for its AI compute platform `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json] TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=57006000000 net_income=31910000000 revenue_yoy=+62.5% revenue_qoq=+22.0%`. Net income grew 65.3% year-over-year to $31.9 billion, with net margins expanding to 56.0% in the quarter `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json] TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=57006000000 net_income=31910000000 ... net_income_yoy=+65.3% ... net_margin=+56.0%`. These figures reflect sustained pricing power and ecosystem lock-in.

However, this growth is increasingly capital-inefficient and exposed to inventory risk. NVDA recorded $378 million in inventory provisions during FY2026Q3, up from $322 million in the same quarter a year prior, and a staggering $3.6 billion in provisions for the first nine months of FY2026 — more than four times the $876 million incurred in the prior-year period `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm] We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively, and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively`. This surge in provisions signals either overproduction, product transition risk, or weakening channel demand — all of which undermine the sustainability of current margins.

Despite aggressive buybacks — $9.7 billion in stock repurchased during FY2026Q2 alone — capital returns have not offset investor concerns about valuation and competitive evolution `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm] of our common stock for $ 9.7 billion and $ 7.0 billion during the second quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively`. The company remains authorized to repurchase up to $14.7 billion in shares as of July 27, 2025 `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm] As of July 27, 2025, we were authorized, subject to certain specifications, to repurchase up to $ 14.7 billion of our common stock`. Yet, this capital discipline is inconsistent: while dividends rose slightly year-over-year (from $344 million to $488 million in the first half), the disproportionate focus on buybacks amid record inventory charges suggests financial engineering is supplementing, not reinforcing, operational strength.

The market has begun to price in this divergence. NVDA’s share price rose 34.8% from January 2 to December 31, 2025 `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv] TOOL price_event_tool NVDA 2025-01-02 to 2025-12-31 start_close=138.31 end_close=186.5 close_return=+34.8%`, but this return lagged the peer average of 63.4%, resulting in a relative alpha of -28.5% `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv; prices/AVGO.csv,prices/AMD.csv] TOOL peer_relative_tool NVDA subject_return=+34.8% peer_average_return=+63.4% relative_alpha=-28.5%`. Notably, AMD — a direct competitor in AI accelerators — delivered 77.5% returns, while Broadcom (AVGO), a key supplier to AI hyperscalers, returned 49.2% `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv; prices/AVGO.csv,prices/AMD.csv] TOOL peer_relative_tool ... best_peer=AMD:+77.5% worst_peer=AVGO:+49.2%`. This underperformance occurred despite NVDA’s superior EPS growth — EPS of $1.30 in Q3 beat estimates by $0.0254 (1.99%) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json] NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)` — suggesting the market is no longer rewarding top-line growth at previous multiples.

Sentiment has shifted. The “OpenAI complex” — a basket including NVDA, AMD, Microsoft, and CoreWeave — surged 90–100% by early October 2025, but stalled thereafter `[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-25.json] From late 2024 through mid‑2025 the 2 baskets traded in a tight band ... ramping to roughly +40‑50% by July and to about +90‑100% by early October`. By late November, NVDA was “getting punished” even as peers like AVGO hit all-time highs `[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json] $NVDA getting punished. ... Meanwhile… $AVGO smashed a new ATH before earnings`. This divergence reflects investor rotation toward companies with clearer margin visibility and less exposure to AI capex cyclicality.

Cross-source evidence confirms a decoupling between fundamentals and valuation. Strong TTM net income of $96.4 billion and a 52.7% TTM net margin `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json] TOOL ratio_calc_tool NVDA TTM through FY2026Q3 revenue_ttm=182893000000 net_income_ttm=96416000000 net_margin_ttm=+52.7%` are offset by a 49.6% annualized volatility and a -36.9% max drawdown in 2025 `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv] TOOL price_event_tool NVDA 2025-01-02 to 2025-12-31 ... max_drawdown=-36.9% annualized_volatility=+49.6%`. Such volatility is inconsistent with a “quality growth” premium and more typical of a momentum-driven, macro-sensitive asset.

**Falsification condition:** This thesis would be invalidated if NVDA’s inventory provisions declined meaningfully in FY2026Q4 while revenue growth remained above 50% year-over-year and gross margins expanded beyond 73.4% — evidence of both demand strength and supply chain efficiency. Absent such a result, the current valuation embeds unrealistic assumptions about margin durability and competitive insulation.

NVDA remains a leader in AI compute, but its stock now prices in perfection. With peer underperformance, rising inventory risk, and capital returns failing to close the confidence gap, the path of least resistance is lower. **We rate NVDA as Overweight no longer; the evidence supports a Neutral to Underweight stance for 2026.**
