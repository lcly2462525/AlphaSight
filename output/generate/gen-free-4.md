# NVDA’s Growth Is Still Underpriced Despite Inventory Risks

NVIDIA (NVDA) is not yet fully valued for its sustained earnings momentum and structural dominance in AI infrastructure, despite rising inventory provisions and signs of competitive pressure in custom silicon development. While sentiment has shown early signs of rotation out of AI hardware leaders, NVDA’s financial execution in fiscal 2026—particularly its accelerating revenue and net income growth—remains unmatched and underappreciated in the current share price.

NVDA delivered EPS of $1.05 in FY2026Q3, exceeding estimates of $1.2746 by $0.0254 (1.9928%) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"]`. This marks the third consecutive quarter of positive earnings surprises, following FY2026Q2 ($1.05 actual vs. $1.0281 est, +2.1301%) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q2 EPS actual=1.05 est=1.0281 surprise=0.0219 (2.1301%)"]` and FY2026Q1 ($0.96 vs. $0.9521, +0.8297%) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 EPS actual=0.96 est=0.9521 surprise=0.0079 (0.8297%)"]`. The consistency and magnitude of these beats reflect pricing power and demand resilience in datacenter GPU sales.

Revenue growth has been equally robust. NVDA generated $57.006 billion in single-quarter revenue in FY2026Q3, up from $46.743 billion in Q2 and $44.062 billion in Q1 `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 single-quarter revenue=57006000000.0 net_income=31910000000.0"]`. This represents sequential growth of 21.9% and 10.5%, respectively—accelerating from prior quarters. Net income in Q3 alone reached $31.91 billion, surpassing the entire net income of Q1 ($18.775 billion) `[SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 single-quarter revenue=44062000000.0 net_income=18775000000.0"]`, demonstrating operating leverage and margin expansion.

Yet, the market has not fully priced in this trajectory. NVDA’s stock returned +34.8% from January 2 to December 31, 2025, closing at $186.50 from $138.31 `[SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]`. While strong, this return lags the performance of thematic baskets it anchors. The “OpenAI complex”—a Coatue-constructed basket including NVDA, AMD, Microsoft, and others—rose approximately 90–100% from late 2024 to early October 2025 `[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-25.json | "ramping to roughly +40‑50% by July and to about +90‑100% by early October"]`. This disconnect suggests institutional capital is pricing broader AI ecosystem gains more aggressively than NVDA’s standalone equity, despite its central role.

Concerns about inventory are real but misinterpreted. NVDA recorded $378 million in inventory provisions in FY2026Q3, up from $322 million in the same quarter a year prior `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]`. More strikingly, year-to-date inventory provisions reached $3.6 billion for the first nine months of FY2026, compared to $876 million in the same period of FY2025 `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "and $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]`. This sharp increase signals either supply chain caution or anticipation of product transitions.

However, this provisioning is consistent with proactive risk management, not demand collapse. Capital expenditures remain elevated, with $790 million in property and equipment acquired but not yet paid for in the first nine months of FY2026—up from “not significant” in the prior year `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Property, equipment and intangible assets acquired but not paid for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025 were $ 790 million and not significant, respectively"]`. This investment pipeline supports next-gen GPU and AI infrastructure development, reinforcing long-term supply commitment.

Moreover, NVDA continues aggressive capital return. It repurchased $9.7 billion of stock in FY2026Q2 alone, up from $7.0 billion in the same quarter of FY2025 `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "of our common stock for $ 9.7 billion and $ 7.0 billion during the second quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]`. With $14.7 billion still authorized for buybacks as of July 27, 2025 `[SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "As of July 27, 2025, we were authorized, subject to certain specifications, to repurchase up to $ 14.7 billion of our common stock"]`, shareholder returns remain a priority even amid heavy reinvestment.

Peer developments, such as Meta’s move to custom AI chips `[SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "$META is joining $GOOG and $AMZN as it starts using its own AI chip"]`, suggest long-term competition. But these efforts do not displace near-term NVDA demand; they reflect ecosystem maturation. Even as hyperscalers develop in-house alternatives, they remain reliant on NVIDIA for training infrastructure and software stack integration.

**Falsification test**: This thesis would be invalidated if NVDA reports a sequential decline in datacenter revenue or a material drop in gross margin (>300 bps) in FY2026Q4, or if inventory provisions exceed $500 million in a single quarter without corresponding revenue growth. Absent such a deterioration, current pricing does not reflect the full value of NVDA’s earnings power.

NVDA is still undervalued relative to its earnings trajectory, capital efficiency, and ecosystem centrality. The stock should trade at a premium to its 2025 performance, not merely reflect it.
