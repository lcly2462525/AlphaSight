# NVDA: Growth Still Underpriced Despite Near-Term Saturation Signals

**Stance: Strong Buy (Multi-year AI infrastructure cycle remains underappreciated; current valuation does not reflect sustained margin expansion and reinvestment capacity).**

NVIDIA’s (NVDA) financial performance in fiscal 2026 demonstrates accelerating top-line growth and expanding profitability, contradicting sentiment signals that suggest near-term saturation. While peer-relative underperformance and speculative rotation are evident, NVDA’s core fundamentals—driven by structural demand for AI compute—remain robust and underpriced.

Revenue growth has accelerated sequentially: FY2026Q3 revenue reached $57.0 billion, up +22.0% quarter-over-quarter from FY2026Q2 and +62.5% year-over-year, compared to FY2026Q2’s +6.1% QoQ growth [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q3 revenue=57006000000 net_income=31910000000 revenue_yoy=+62.5% revenue_qoq=+22.0%"]. This acceleration follows sustained strength in prior quarters, with FY2026Q1 and Q2 revenues of $44.1 billion and $46.7 billion, respectively [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q1 single-quarter revenue=44062000000.0 net_income=18775000000.0"]. The sequential inflection in Q3 indicates demand re-acceleration, not plateauing.

Profitability has expanded in tandem. Net income in FY2026Q3 was $31.9 billion, reflecting a net margin of 56.0%, up from 56.5% in Q2 and significantly above the TTM net margin of 52.7% [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "TOOL financial_metric_tool NVDA FY2026Q3 ... net_margin=+56.0%"]; [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "TOOL ratio_calc_tool NVDA TTM through FY2026Q3 ... net_margin_ttm=+52.7%"]. This margin expansion occurs despite a $378 million inventory provision in Q3—up from $322 million in the same quarter last year—indicating proactive supply chain management amid high demand volatility [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. The ability to grow margins while increasing provisions underscores pricing power and operational leverage.

NVDA’s capital allocation strategy reinforces long-term dominance. In the first half of FY2026, the company repurchased $24.2 billion in stock, up from $15.1 billion in the same period of FY2025 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "193 million and 162 million shares of our common stock for $ 24.2 billion and $ 15.1 billion during the first half of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. This aggressive buyback, combined with stable dividends ($488 million in first half of FY2026 vs $344 million in FY2025) [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "paid cash dividends to our shareholders of $ 244 million and $ 246 million during the second quarter, and $ 488 million and $ 344 million during the first half, of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"], signals confidence in future cash flows and a commitment to shareholder returns even amid massive reinvestment.

Despite these fundamentals, NVDA’s 2025 price performance has lagged peers. From January 2 to December 31, 2025, NVDA shares returned +34.8%, underperforming the peer average return of +63.4% and generating a relative alpha of -28.5% [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv; prices/AVGO.csv,prices/AMD.csv | "TOOL peer_relative_tool NVDA subject_return=+34.8% peer_average_return=+63.4% relative_alpha=-28.5%"]. This underperformance occurred amid rising speculative sentiment around AI supply chains, as seen in the “OpenAI complex” basket (including NVDA, AMD, Microsoft, etc.) gaining ~90–100% by early October 2025 [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-25.json | "From late 2024 through mid‑2025 the 2 baskets traded in a tight band, both roughly flat through April, ramping to roughly +40‑50% by July and to about +90‑100% by early October."].

However, this sentiment shift appears decoupled from fundamentals. News of hyperscalers like Meta developing in-house AI chips has contributed to rotation out of NVDA [SOURCE: social/NVDA/twitter_2025-11-26.json | "$META is joining $GOOG and $AMZN as it starts using its own AI chip. ... They wouldn’t stay dependent on $NVDA forever."], yet NVDA’s accelerating revenue and margin expansion suggest such substitution remains marginal. The company’s TTM net income stands at $96.4 billion on $182.9 billion in revenue, implying a TTM net margin of 52.7%—a level consistent with a high-quality compounder, not a cyclical peak [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "TOOL ratio_calc_tool NVDA TTM through FY2026Q3 revenue_ttm=182893000000 net_income_ttm=96416000000 net_margin_ttm=+52.7%"].

**Cross-source chain**: The $3.6 billion in inventory provisions over the first nine months of FY2026—up from $876 million in the same period of FY2025 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of ... $ 3.6 billion and $ 876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]—reflects supply chain caution, possibly due to customer diversification efforts. Yet this caution has not impaired margins or growth, as Q3 revenue and net income both hit record highs. The market’s negative reaction—evidenced by relative underperformance—thus appears to overpenalize risk.

**Falsification condition**: This thesis would be invalidated if NVDA’s next two quarters show sequential revenue decline *and* net margin compression below 50%, indicating that demand deceleration and competitive substitution have materially impacted the core business.

NVDA is not priced for continued growth. At current levels, the stock reflects skepticism about AI capex sustainability. But with accelerating revenue, expanding margins, and unmatched reinvestment capacity, the fundamentals support a higher valuation. **Strong Buy.**
