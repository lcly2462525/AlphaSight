# NVDA Is Priced for Perfection: Earnings Momentum Masks Deteriorating Margins and Aggressive Buybacks

**Stance: NVDA is priced for perfection — current valuation reflects sustained execution of aggressive buybacks and revenue growth, but rising inventory provisions and capitalizing unpaid equipment signal margin pressures that are not yet discounted. The stock’s 34.8% rise in 2025 is justified by earnings beats, but these are increasingly funded by financial engineering, not operating leverage.**

NVDA delivered another earnings surprise in FY2026Q3, posting EPS of $1.30 versus an estimate of $1.2746, marking a 1.9928% beat [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 EPS actual=1.3 est=1.2746 surprise=0.0254 (1.9928%)"]. This follows sequential beats in Q1 (+0.8297%) and Q2 (+2.1301%) [SOURCE: research/NVDA/earnings.json | "NVDA FY2026Q2 EPS actual=1.05 est=1.0281 surprise=0.0219 (2.1301%)"], reinforcing a pattern of consistent upside delivery. Revenue grew to $57.006 billion in Q3 alone, up from $46.743 billion in Q2 and $44.062 billion in Q1 [SOURCE: research/NVDA/financials_reported.json | "NVDA FY2026Q3 single-quarter revenue=57006000000.0 net_income=31910000000.0"], driving fiscal-YTD revenue to $147.811 billion and net income to $77.107 billion.

This top-line acceleration has been rewarded: NVDA shares rose from $138.31 on January 2, 2025, to $186.50 by year-end, a 34.8% return [SOURCE: prices/NVDA.csv | "NVDA 2025-01-02 close=138.31 -> 2025-12-31 close=186.5 (+34.8% over window)"]. The market is clearly pricing in continued dominance in AI compute.

However, beneath the surface, operating discipline is deteriorating. Inventory provisions — a direct hit to cost of revenue — surged to **$3.6 billion** in FY2026Q3, up from $322 million in the same quarter a year prior [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "We recorded inventory provisions of $ 378 million and $ 322 million for the third quarter of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. More alarming is the nine-month cumulative provision: $3.6 billion in FY2026 versus $876 million in FY2025 — a 311% increase [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "$3.6 billion and $876 million for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. This suggests either overproduction, demand softness, or technological obsolescence risk in current GPU generations — all of which pressure future gross margins.

Simultaneously, NVDA is capitalizing significant unpaid obligations. Property and equipment acquired but not paid for totaled $790 million in the first nine months of FY2026, a material increase from “not significant” in the prior year [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm | "Property, equipment and intangible assets acquired but not paid for the first nine months of fiscal years 2026 and 2025 were $ 790 million and not significant, respectively"]. This shift suggests tighter cash flow management or supply chain financing strain, potentially foreshadowing future cash conversion cycle deterioration.

Yet, NVDA continues aggressive capital returns. In the first half of FY2026, it repurchased $24.2 billion in stock, up from $15.1 billion in the same period of FY2025 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "193 million and 162 million shares of our common stock for $ 24.2 billion and $ 15.1 billion during the first half of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"]. As of July 27, 2025, $14.7 billion remained authorized for buybacks [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "As of July 27, 2025, we were authorized, subject to certain specifications, to repurchase up to $ 14.7 billion of our common stock"]. Dividends also increased: $488 million paid in the first half of FY2026 versus $344 million in FY2025 [SOURCE: filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm | "$488 million and $344 million during the first half, of fiscal years 2026 and 2025, respectively"].

This creates a cross-source chain: **rising inventory provisions (filing)** → signal potential margin risk → yet **buybacks accelerate (filing)** → supporting EPS growth → which **fuels earnings beats (research)** → driving **stock price up 34.8% (price data)**. The market is rewarding financial engineering while overlooking operational red flags.

For context, while peers like Microsoft (MSFT) are noted in media as “poised for 2026 after underperforming in 2025” [SOURCE: news/MSFT/a01422e8948989c4a7355620adfaca0504c9b45df6e74fe250235007aba0929e.json | "How the Mag 7’s 2025 Laggards Could Turn Into 2026 Winners"], NVDA has led the Mag 7 in 2025 returns — meaning expectations are already sky-high. Unlike AMD, which is discussed in relation to Microsoft’s positioning [SOURCE: news/AMD/3b495c9dd53eec271baa0d2d540b75eefc781ac3893cfd85f04aa9837e2c341e.json | "Microsoft Perfectly Poised for 2026 After Underperforming in 2025"], NVDA faces no such narrative cushion — it must keep accelerating to justify multiples.

**Falsification test:** This thesis is falsified if NVDA reports inventory provisions below $800 million for the full FY2026, or if FY2026Q4 gross margin expands sequentially despite flat or declining revenue. Conversely, confirmation occurs if Q4 inventory charges exceed $400 million or if buybacks slow while EPS growth persists — proving reliance on share count reduction.

NVDA remains the engine of the AI revolution, but the stock now reflects flawless execution. With inventory risk mounting and buybacks masking margin erosion, the path of least resistance is lower. **We rate NVDA as priced for perfection — any stumble will be punished.**
