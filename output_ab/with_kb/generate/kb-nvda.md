# **NVIDIA FY2026 Q3 Earnings Analysis: Beat, Revenue Momentum, and Forward Outlook**

## **Summary**

NVIDIA delivered a significant earnings beat in its fiscal 2026 third quarter (ended October 26, 2025), driven by explosive growth in its Data Center segment. The company reported revenue of $57.0 billion and diluted EPS of $1.30, surpassing consensus expectations. This performance reflects sustained demand for AI infrastructure, particularly Blackwell-based systems, and a strategic shift from discrete GPU sales to full-stack rack-scale solutions. Year-to-date (YTD) revenue reached $147.8 billion, with Q3 alone contributing $57.0 billion—indicating accelerating momentum. Looking ahead, strong execution, robust cash generation, and leadership in scalable AI platforms support a positive price return outlook for 2025, despite elevated valuations.

## **Key Findings**

### **EPS Surprise and Beat Direction**
NVIDIA significantly exceeded expectations in FY2026 Q3. While specific analyst consensus figures are not available in the filings, external sources indicate expectations of approximately $1.25 in EPS and $54.8 billion in revenue [news/AMD/8e1dc9da1dd0951a46b67d54abe36c83a0081884a6bd88bde86fa1994dd47881.json]. The company reported diluted EPS of $1.30 and revenue of $57.0 billion, representing a clear beat on both metrics [filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. This outperformance was fueled by a 74% year-over-year increase in revenue and a 65% increase in net income, demonstrating exceptional top-line growth and margin resilience.

### **Single-Quarter vs. YTD Revenue**
The de-cumulated Q3 revenue of $57.0 billion represents a substantial acceleration from prior quarters. This compares to $46.7 billion in Q2 (July 27, 2025) and $35.1 billion in Q3 of the prior year [filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm; filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-08-27__0001045810-25-000209.htm]. The sequential increase of $10.3 billion is particularly notable, as it exceeds the entire quarterly revenue of competitors like AMD. This surge contributed to a YTD revenue of $147.8 billion, up from $91.2 billion in the same period last year. The disproportionate contribution from Q3 underscores an accelerating growth trajectory, likely driven by the ramp of Blackwell GPU deployments and full AI rack sales.

### **Segment Performance and Market Position**
The Data Center segment, which includes Compute and Networking, is the primary growth engine. While the 10-Q does not break out Data Center revenue for Q3, external analysis confirms it exceeded $51 billion for the quarter, a 66% year-over-year increase [news/AAPL/4605a81b0d161cfefac9f4e5c48b9673d7d53bc71e7051ee9fd25c610b05e08b.json]. This growth is attributed to a shift from selling individual GPUs to integrated systems like the NVL72 rack, which leverages fifth-generation NVLink for superior scalability. This platform-level advantage creates a significant moat, as competitors struggle to achieve similar performance at scale.

### **2025 Price Return Outlook**
NVIDIA’s stock performance in 2025 is supported by its execution and market dominance. The company’s ability to deliver consistent, accelerating growth has solidified its position as the leader in the AI infrastructure market. Forward-looking DCF models, incorporating projected revenue CAGRs of ~37.7% and EBITDA margins of 64–65%, suggest a fair value range of $241–$258 per share, implying 35–45% upside from a $177.91 price point [news/AAPL/4605a81b0d161cfefac9f4e5c48b9673d7d53bc71e7051ee9fd25c610b05e08b.json]. Longer-term estimates extend into the $355–$487 range under full execution. The stock’s behavior as a "premium compounder" with high revenue elasticity (0.28) suggests that continued growth surprises will drive significant positive returns.

## **Risks**

- **Supply Chain Constraints:** The primary bottleneck for growth has shifted from demand to supply, specifically TSMC wafer capacity and networking hardware availability [news/AAPL/4605a81b0d161cfefac9f4e5c48b9673d7d53bc71e7051ee9fd25c610b05e08b.json]. Any disruption in the supply chain could limit the company’s ability to fulfill orders.
- **Geopolitical Tensions:** U.S. export restrictions on advanced AI chips to China remain a headwind. While there have been policy shifts, Chinese government policies actively discourage reliance on U.S. technology, capping the realistic revenue opportunity from this market [news/AAPL/4605a81b0d161cfefac9f4e5c48b9673d7d53bc71e7051ee9fd25c610b05e08b.json].
- **Valuation and Expectations:** The stock trades at a premium valuation (e.g., ~23x price-to-sales), reflecting high expectations [news/AAPL/4605a81b0d161cfefac9f4e5c48b9673d7d53bc71e7051ee9fd25c610b05e08b.json]. Any sign of growth deceleration or failure to meet aggressive guidance could trigger a sharp correction.
- **Competition:** While NVIDIA holds a dominant position, competitors like AMD and consortia such as UA Link are investing heavily to close the gap in scalable AI infrastructure, posing a long-term threat.

## **Forward View**

NVIDIA is poised to maintain its leadership in the AI compute market through fiscal 2026 and beyond. The company’s transition to a platform-based model, selling full AI racks rather than discrete chips, has created a durable competitive advantage. The massive $10.3 billion sequential revenue increase in Q3 demonstrates that demand remains robust and is not yet saturated. Forward guidance, while not detailed in the 10-Q, is expected to be strong, given the persistent order backlog and hyperscaler investment trends.

The company’s financial strength is evident in its balance sheet, with cash and marketable securities totaling $60.6 billion as of October 26, 2025, and a strong cash flow generation profile [filings/NVDA/10-Q__2025-11-19__0001045810-25-000230.htm]. This provides ample flexibility for R&D, strategic acquisitions, and shareholder returns.

For 2025, the price return is likely to be positive, driven by continued earnings outperformance and upward revisions to long-term growth estimates. However, returns will be highly sensitive to execution and guidance. The market is no longer pricing in hope but is demanding sustained, flawless execution. As long as NVIDIA can navigate supply constraints and deliver on its platform vision, it remains well-positioned for significant outperformance.
